Ben Simmons

Robles
Ben Simmons was the first overall pick to the 76ers in the 2016 NBA draft, and he had high expectations to be a great player. He had a great start to his career. He didn't play in the 2016-17 season due to injury, but played the 2017-18 season, winning rookie of the year. Following this, he made the all star team in 2019. This led to a massive payday, earning a 5 year, $177 million contract over the 2019 offseason. He made the all star team in 2020 and 2021, showing no signs of regression. However, this did not last for long. It all started when Ben Simmons passed up a wide open layup in game seven of the 76ers second round playoff series against the Atlanta Hawks in 2021. He was mocked by the media and felt disgruntled with the situation in Philidelphia. He didn't play for the 76ers in the 2021-22 season as he requested a trade, and he went to the Brooklyn Nets. He didn't play for the rest of the 2021-22 season due to mental issues, but people had high hopes going into the 2022-23 season. He posted an offseason video showing improvement, but played very badly to start the season and soon got injured. He didn't play for the rest of the season, and had this same trajectory going into the 2023-24 season. This is what became the Ben Simmons cycle. To this day. He is still one of the most clowned NBA players and is known for his generational falloff.
